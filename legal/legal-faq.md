### **Q1: Does the Living Vote Protocol replace elections?**

**No.**

The Living Vote Protocol does not replace elections, parliaments, or constitutional procedures.
It models a **continuous legitimacy signal** that can exist alongside periodic elections.

Elections remain legally untouched.

---

### **Q2: Is this a form of direct democracy?**

**No.**

The protocol does not execute decisions or votes.
It derives **legitimacy signals**, not binding outcomes.

Direct democracy requires enforceable decisions.
The Living Vote Protocol explicitly avoids that.

---

### **Q3: Does the protocol violate representative democracy?**

**No.**

Representative institutions remain fully intact.
The protocol only provides **additional information** about current public legitimacy.

No mandate is withdrawn.
No authority is overridden.

---

### **Q4: Who is legally responsible for decisions derived from the protocol?**

**The decision-maker, not the protocol.**

The protocol produces no administrative acts and no legal obligations.
Responsibility remains with existing political or institutional actors.

---

### **Q5: Is this legally binding?**

**No.**

The protocol has **no binding legal force** by itself.
Any binding use would require explicit legal adoption.

---

### **Q6: Does the protocol violate the separation of powers?**

**No.**

Because it does not decide or enforce outcomes,
it does not interfere with legislative, executive, or judicial authority.

---

### **Q7: Is citizen participation mandatory?**

**No.**

Participation is voluntary.
Non-participation does not reduce rights or representation.

---

### **Q8: Does this system require digital identity or surveillance?**

**No.**

The protocol separates:

* identity ownership
* from public aggregation

The core proof does not require authentication, cryptography, or public identity disclosure.

---

### **Q9: Is artificial intelligence making political decisions?**

**No.**

The protocol does not require AI.
Any future use of AI would be limited to **analysis**, not decision-making.

---

### **Q10: Is this a political party program or ideology?**

**No.**

The Living Vote Protocol is **procedural**, not ideological.
It defines no goals, values, or policies.

---

### **Summary Statement**

> **The Living Vote Protocol is a technical and procedural model for deriving legitimacy signals.
> It does not assert authority, replace institutions, or create legal obligations.**
