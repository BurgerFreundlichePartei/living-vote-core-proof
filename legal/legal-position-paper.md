# **Legal Position Paper**

## The Living Vote Protocol (LVP)

### Constitutional and Legal Positioning — Phase B2

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Public position paper
**Related Release:** v0.1-core-proof

---

## 1. Purpose of This Paper

This document provides a **legal and constitutional positioning** of the **Living Vote Protocol (LVP)** following the
completion of its **technical core proof**.

Its purpose is to clarify:

* what has been **technically proven**
* how the protocol relates to **democratic and constitutional principles**
* what the protocol **does not claim**
* why no legal contradiction is asserted at this stage

This paper does **not** advocate adoption.
It establishes **legal intelligibility and discourse readiness**.

---

## 2. What Has Been Proven (Scope of Proof)

The completed core proof demonstrates that:

* individual political profiles can exist as persistent legal subjects
* political legitimacy can be derived continuously from current profile configurations
* delegation of political influence can be explicit, reversible, and cycle-safe
* legitimacy aggregation can be deterministic, transparent, and non-arbitrary
* identity ownership and authorization can be enforced at the domain level
* no political decision authority is required for the system to function

These properties are verified by executable tests and documented compliance.

**This is a proof of logical and structural consistency — not of legal validity.**

---

## 3. Fundamental Legal Position

### 3.1 Core Thesis

> **The Living Vote Protocol is not an alternative constitutional order.
> It is a procedural model for deriving legitimacy signals.**

The protocol does **not** claim sovereign authority.
It does **not** replace elections, parliaments, courts, or constitutions.

---

### 3.2 Procedural, Not Normative

The protocol:

* defines no political goals
* enforces no outcomes
* expresses no ideology

It models **procedure**, not **policy**.

This distinction is legally decisive.

---

## 4. Relation to Democratic Principles

### 4.1 Popular Sovereignty

In democratic systems, public authority is derived from the people.

The protocol aligns with this principle by:

* keeping political agency with individual citizens
* preventing permanent alienation of political influence
* allowing continuous reconfiguration of political support

No restriction of popular sovereignty is introduced.

---

### 4.2 Elections and Representation

The protocol does **not** abolish elections.

Instead, it models an **additional, continuous legitimacy layer** that can exist:

* before elections
* between elections
* alongside representative institutions

Elections remain legally intact and unchanged.

---

### 4.3 Equality and Freedom

* Each citizen holds exactly one political profile
* No profile carries inherent weighting advantages
* Participation is voluntary
* Non-participation does not cause loss of rights

These properties are compatible with democratic equality and freedom principles.

---

## 5. Non-Decision Principle and Legal Safety

A central legal safeguard of the protocol is the **non-decision principle**:

> **The Living Vote Protocol derives legitimacy signals but does not execute decisions.**

As a result:

* no administrative act is produced
* no legal obligation is created
* no binding norm is enacted

This prevents conflicts with:

* separation of powers
* administrative law
* constitutional jurisdiction

---

## 6. Identity, Privacy, and Responsibility

The protocol explicitly separates:

* **identity ownership** (citizen ↔ profile)
* from **public visibility** (aggregated outputs only)

No requirement for public identity disclosure is imposed.

The current proof enforces ownership at the domain level without implementing authentication or cryptography, which are
acknowledged as future concerns rather than prerequisites for legal reasoning.

---

## 7. What the Protocol Does *Not* Claim

The Living Vote Protocol does **not** claim that:

* it is legally binding
* it is constitutionally valid by default
* it should replace existing institutions
* it produces better political outcomes
* it resolves societal conflict

All such claims would require political, legal, and societal processes beyond this proof.

---

## 8. Legal Implications of the Proof

The existence of a functioning, testable core implies that:

* continuous legitimacy modeling is **technically possible**
* reversibility of political delegation is **structurally feasible**
* democratic participation does not require periodic electoral events alone

These implications open legal and political questions — they do not answer them.
